Overview

In this trick-taking game, players decide when to win tricks and when to increase the value of the number of tricks they think they will take. After 3 rounds, the player with the most points wins!

Deck Makeup

  • 63 Monster Cards (0-7 in 7 colors)

Note: the Trickster's Table implementation of this game uses standard 4-player rules but the physcial version supports 2-5 players and has variant rules. See the official rulebook for more details.

Round Flow

  1. Light a Torch: Each player selects a card face down as their torch (a guess at how many tricks they'll win).
  2. Play Tricks: Players play cards clockwise, following suit if able. Dragon cards allow free play. Determine the winning number (highest dragon or highest of lead color).
  3. Move Adventurers: All who played the winning number advance one dungeon. From dungeon 7, move to 0.
  4. Spawn Monsters: Player who played the lowest number becomes Dungeon Warden and places cards into matching-numbered dungeons (one per color).

Final Trick

Before the final trick, players take back their torch card. No suit-following requirement this round. Proceed with trick as usual.

Relight Torches

Reveal the unplayed card. If its number matches your dungeon location, place it face down as a relit torch (worth bonus). Otherwise, place it in that dungeon as a dropped torch card.

Scoring

  • 2 points per dropped torch card
  • 2 points per dragon card
  • 1 point per other monster card
  • Divide dungeon score among all players in the same dungeon (rounded down)
  • +3 points for relighting your torch

Prepare Next Round

  • Return pawns to dungeon 0
  • Shuffle and redeal cards
  • New round starts with previous Dungeon Warden

End of Game

After 3 rounds, the player with the highest total score wins. In a tie, the player with the highest score in round 3 wins. Still tied? Share victory. (Note: in the Trickster's Table implementation, if the human player's score after 3 rounds is tied for the highest score it is counted as a win for the human player. Don't tell the bots.)
