Best of Neapolitan Rules

Support the Best of Neapolitan Kickstarter by the Portland Game Collective and Travel-Games.co.uk

Best of Neapolitan a trick taking game for 3-5 players designed by Marco Jung. The game is always played with 4 players in Trickster's Table. The scoring and card distribution changes depending on player count so if you are playing with 3 or 5 be sure to look up the official rules.

Play (4 players)

  • Best of Neapolitan is played with a deck that consists of 3 suits each containing cards valued 1 through 9 and three suitless pass cards.
  • Each player is dealt 7 cards and the remaining 2 cards are placed face up.
  • Trump is displayed at the top left of the screen. The order of trump is randomly determined before each hand and is heirarchical. Any card from the topmost trump will beat any card from the middle trump and so on. After each trick, the suit of the winning card will move to the bottom of the heirarchy.
  • Best of Neapolitan is a must-follow game. When leading, you may play any card. If a card has been led you must follow its suit if you can. The exception to this rule is pass cards which may be played at any time (even if you could have followed suit). Pass cards cannot win a trick.

Scoring (4 players)

Players score based on how many tricks they took in a round.

  • Players that took exactly 2 tricks score 1 point.
  • Players that took exactly 0 tricks score 2 points.
  • Any other trick count scores 0 points.

Objective

The objective of Best of Neapolitan is to get to 5 points first. If there is a tie for first place additional rounds are played until there is a clear winner (the original game has different tie breaker rules than the app implementation).
