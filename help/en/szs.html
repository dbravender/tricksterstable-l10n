Theme

Welcome to 1935 Bollywoods! It's Oscar night and the stars have come to walk the red carpet, or rather crawl. See this is Bollywoods, Bear Hollywood, and these osos are oh so stylish. Thankfully, Goldilocks has agreed to put the past in the past and make you just the right fit for your zoot suit-not too long and not too short. Now try your darndest to be the Bear of the Ball and win beast dressed!

The Hook

A trick-taking game where players will take advantage of their own personal draw deck to be short-suited at the same rate as they win tricks.

Setup

In Trickster's Table Short Zoot Suit is played with 3 players. Cards are valued 1-12 in 4 suits.

Gameplay Flow

At the start of a round, players pick any 5 cards from the cards they were dealt. Players then flip these 5 cards facedown, shuffle them, and place them into a pile in front of themselves. This is the draw pile for the player. Before every trick begins, anyone may draw 1 card from their personal draw deck. If, after this, anyone has 0 cards left in their hand (not draw deck), the round is over. The trick-taking is must follow, with no trump. The highest card of the lead suit wins. The winner takes the trick, except for any non-lead-suit cards. Those cards go back to the player who played them, face-up into that player's shorts pile.

Scoring

Players earn 1 point per trick won and 3 points for each pair of won trick and card in their shorts pile. If the number of tricks they won equals the number of shorts, the player earns 5 points per pair rather than just 3 points.

End of Round

Players play rounds per player, where the next round’ s start player is the person to the left of the previous round’ s start player.

End of Game

Most points wins! Congrats on being best dressed!

If tied, the tied players select a movie from a randomly generated list of Taylor's favorite movies plus some movies that aren't his favorite. The player who selected Taylor's highest ranked movie wins!
