Yokai Septet - 2 player variant

Yokai Septet is a 2-4 player trick taking game designed by Muneyuki Yokouchi. The two-player variant was designed by Sean Ross. It appears in Trickster's Table with the permission of Ninja Star Games (thank you Dan Kobayashi!).

Game Overview

You want to capture 4 or more Boss Yokai (7s) to win a round. You will lose the round if you win 13 tricks. If you play all 23 tricks, then the player who won the last trick wins the round.

Card Distributions

Round Setup

The deck of 49 cards is shuffled. 7 cards are dealt face-down to each player. 6 cards are placed face-up on top of the previous 7 cards. These piles are called the straw piles.

Next, 11 cards are dealt face-down to each player. One card is dealt face-up which determines the Trump Suit for the round.

Finally, each player discards one card from their hand face-down. You may not discard a Boss Yokai. The discarded cards are not used this round.

Before the Round Begins

If you have Boss Yokai cards face-up in your straw pile, you may choose to swap one of them with one of the two cards beneath it. You may not look at the face-down cards before deciding to swap. If the face-down card you chose is also a Boss Yokai, then you must choose which one to keep face-up and which one to turn face-down. (Please note that in the Trickster's Table implementation, Boss Yokai cards will not appear as face-up cards on the straw pile.)

The player who dealt the cards leads the first trick. In subsequent rounds, swap the dealer so that the first lead changes each round.

Game Play Rules

Each round, you'll play multiple turns called Tricks where each player will play one card from their hand. The Lead Player will play first. They choose any card from their hand and play it face-up in front of them. The other player must now play a card of the same suit as the Lead Player. If a player has no cards of that suit, they may play any card in their hand.

Taking a trick

After both players have played a card, check to see if either played the A card. If they did, that player takes the trick.

If neither played the A card, check if either played a card of the Trump Suit. If either did, then the higher card of that suit takes the trick.

Otherwise, if both players played the same suit then the higher card takes the trick. If two different suits were played then the Lead Player takes the trick.

The player who took the trick now becomes the Lead Player.

End of a Round

The round is over, and a round winner is determined, if any of the following has happened.

  • A player who has captured a total of 4 or more Boss Yokai cards wins the round.
  • A player has taken 13 tricks but has captured 3 or fewer Boss Yokai cards. The other player wins the round.
  • No one has cards left in their hands. The player who won the last trick wins the round.

Scoring

Only the player who won a round scores points. The other player does not score, regardless of how many cards they took.

If a player has won the round because the other player took 13 tricks, the winning player takes all Boss Yokai cards remaining in both players' hands and the straw piles. They add these to the Boss Yokai they took this round.

The winning player scores as many points as there are white stars on Boss Yokai they captured this round (ignoring the Boss Yokai of the trump suit).

End of the Game

If a player has 7 or more points they win!

Otherwise, a new hand is dealt and another round is played. The lead alternates between players.
