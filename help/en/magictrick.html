Rules explaining how the game is played in real life can be found on the Magic Trick Board Game Geek page.

Deck Composition

  • 56 cards
  • 7 Suits, 0-7 in Each Suit, With Different Colored Backs

Setup

The deck is shuffled and dealt. Each player receives 14 cards. Cards are sorted by value from high to low. For the top and bottom player, this means cards are sorted from top left (low) to bottom right (high). For the left and right player cards are sorted from top to bottom top left is low and bottom right is high.

Gameplay

The player to the left of the dealer starts the hand. They play a card of their choosing into the trick, revealing it face up. Players then play into this trick in clockwise order. Each player will play one card on their turn, and they must follow suit if they can. If they cannot follow, they can play any card. The winner of the trick is the player playing the highest trump card; otherwise, it is the highest card of the leading suit. The red suit (hearts) is always trump. The winner puts the cards in front of them and leads the next trick. Play will continue until all cards have been played.

After playing a card into a trick, but before the next player plays, a player may also put a card "up their sleeve."" They put it face up on the table for all to see (including themselves), and then their bid counter is updated to show this value for the rest of the hand. All players must put one card (but never more) up their sleeve at some point in the hand immediately after playing a card; otherwise, their last card when the hand ends will go up their sleeve. Cards up a sleeve cannot be played into a trick.

Remember: a player can only put a card up their sleeve on their turn, after they have already played into the trick. Thus, if a player's plan was to put a card up their sleeve, but it is their only card that they have to follow suit, they must play it to the trick instead.

Scoring: Bid Scoring: Players earn three points if the face value of the card up their sleeve matches the number of tricks they took, including the 0 bet. Otherwise, they lose one point for the difference between the face value and the number of tricks they took. (For example, if a player took 1 or 5 tricks, and the card up their sleeve had a rank of 3, they'd lose 2 points.)

Prestige Bonus: Players earn an additional two-point bonus if they met their bid and if the number of suits they took in their tricks matches the rank of the card up their sleeve. There is no penalty for missing the prestige bonus. This bonus also scores for the 0 bet. Players can choose to play without the prestige bonus, but it is recommended for advanced players. (Note: this rule is enabled in the Trickster's Table implementation.)

Additional Hands and Victory Condition: Play hands equal to the number of players, rotating the dealer (and thus the start player) clockwise each hand. Record the scores after each hand. High score wins at the end of the game. In the event of a tie, the tied player who received the prestige bonus more times wins. If playing without the prestige bonus, the player among the tied players who hit their bid with the fewest tricks in the last round wins. If there is still a tie, victory is shared.
